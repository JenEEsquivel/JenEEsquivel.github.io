    Jennifer's Short Bio

    Jennifer was born and raised in Wisconsin. She has lived around Wisconsin, in Knoxville, Tennessee, and in Central and South America.

    In Costa Rica, she completed her undergrad studies on a full scholarship for an intensive study-abroad program in tropical ecology. She planned her undergraduate work so that she could finish her final undergrad semester abroad and return to start graduate school in Conservation Geography (people/nature studies, community-based conservation and Latin America.) Between her undergraduate and grad studies, she worked in Monteverde as a cloud forest ecotourist guide.

    Jennifer has worked teaching (Extinction of Species) at UW-Madison, worked for the DNR (in Endangered Resources and for parks). She has managed several internet websites and the portal databases of Forests.org (http://forests.org). Jennifer has helped form several non-profits, worked on websites and served on the board of directors for: Rainforest Biodiversity Group (http://rbg.org) and Friends of the Wisconsin Wolf (fofww.org.) In volunteering to maintain websites for the non-profits she co-founded, she became more interested in web development. This interest lead her to the YWeb Academy to learn more. In addition to working in front-end web development and web technology, she intends on creating another non-profit and website(in her free time) to connect people around the world with crowd-funding for their self-designed conservation projects.
